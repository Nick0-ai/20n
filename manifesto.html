03 The Architecture

The 20n Trinity

Three pillars that fundamentally diverge from the Transformer paradigm.

01

Liquid Neural Networks

Instead of static layers, we use differential equations. The network is "liquid": parameters change during inference, not just training. It adapts to rain, motor failure, unexpected obstacles without retraining. The architecture flows like water.

02

Active Inference (Free Energy Principle)

The AI doesn't maximize reward (slow, unstable RL). 20n minimizes Free Energy (Surprise). It has an internal world model and acts to make reality match predictions. This is how biological brains work—Karl Friston's Free Energy Principle.

03

Spiking Neural Networks (Neuromorphic)

Information transmits only when necessary via "spikes"—like electrical impulses in the brain. Result: 20 Watts (human brain) vs 20,000 Watts (GPU cluster). Computing only when and where it matters.

We don't build an AI that reads.
We build an AI that lives.

04 The Loop

Active Inference Control Loop

The core architecture operates as a closed feedback loop. Unlike feedforward networks, 20n continuously cycles between prediction and sensation, refining its internal world model to minimize surprise and maximize adaptability.

Fig. 1 — Closed-loop perception-action cycle. F = Free Energy.

The system maintains a generative model and continuously predicts incoming sensory data. When predictions don't match reality (surprise), it either updates its model (perception) or acts on the world to make reality match predictions (action).
