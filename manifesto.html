Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) have reached their asymptote. They rest on a false hypothesis: that intelligence is a static compression of past data. This paradigm is energetically unsustainable and structurally incapable of acting in the dynamic physical world.

20n proposes a fundamental paradigm shift: moving from discrete, statistical intelligence (Transformers) to continuous, biological intelligence (Liquid Networks & Active Inference). We don't build an AI that predicts the next word. We build an AI that minimizes entropy to survive and act in the chaos of the real world, with energy efficiency 1000x superior to the state of the art.

Section 02

The 20n Trinity

We base our architecture on three pillars that fundamentally diverge from the Transformer paradigm.

Pillar 01
Liquid Neural Networks & Neural ODEs

Instead of static neuron layers, we use differential equations. The network is "liquid": its parameters change during inference, not just during training. It adapts to new situations (rain, motor failure, unexpected obstacles) without retraining. The architecture flows like water, reshaping itself in real-time.

Pillar 02
Active Inference (Free Energy Principle)

The AI doesn't seek to maximize a "reward" (as others do with Reinforcement Learning, which is slow and unstable). 20n's AI seeks to minimize surprise (free energy). It has an internal model of the world, and it acts to make the world match its model. This is how the biological brain functions.

Pillar 03
Spiking Neural Networks (Neuromorphic)

Information is only transmitted when necessary (via "spikes", like electrical impulses in the brain), not continuously. Result: 20 Watts consumption (human brain) vs 20,000 Watts (GPU cluster). Efficiency through biological mimicry.

Section 03

The Entropy Minimization Loop

The core architecture operates as a closed feedback loop. Unlike feedforward networks that process information in one direction, 20n's system continuously cycles between prediction and sensation, constantly refining its internal world model to minimize surprise.

Fig. 1: The Active Inference Loop

The system maintains a generative model of the world and continuously generates predictions about incoming sensory data. When predictions don't match reality (surprise), the system either updates its model (perception) or acts on the world to make reality match predictions (action).

This bidirectional flow is fundamentally different from traditional neural networks. It creates an agent that doesn't just respond to stimuli but actively seeks to reduce uncertainty about its environment. The result is efficient, adaptive, embodied intelligence.

Section 04

The Protocol: Research Roadmap

From mathematical foundations to embodied synthetic life.

Phase 01

The Silica Worm

Simulation only. Create a simple digital organism (a "worm") capable of learning to move in a complex environment (wind, moving obstacles) with zero pre-training, only through curiosity and energy minimization. Proof of architectural efficiency.

Phase 02

The Ghost in the Shell

Sim2Real transfer (Simulation to Reality). Integration of the 20n brain into a low-cost robotic arm or open-source quadruped (Unitree type). Objective: The robot learns to grasp an unknown object in 10 seconds. (OpenAI takes hours.)

Phase 03

Apex Humanoid

Construction of proprietary body. Biotech collaboration (brain-machine interface). The first synthetic life form capable of continuous learning in unstructured environments.

Objective: 100x less data, 1000x less energy, real-time adaptation.
