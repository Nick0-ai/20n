03

The Vision

Our objective is to create the first synthetic autonomous life form.

→ not science fiction. engineering with a deadline.

Definition of success

An autonomous unit that:

  1. Operates on < 50 Watts continuous
  2. Learns new tasks in < 10 attempts without cloud connectivity
  3. Navigates unstructured environments never seen in training
  4. Costs < $20,000 to manufacture at scale
04

The Technology

We implement what we call the 20n Trinity.

A. Liquid Neural Networks

dx/dt = (−x + f(Wx + b)) / τ(x, input)

The key innovation: τ adapts in real-time based on sensory input. The network remains plastic after deployment.

B. Active Inference

Traditional RL: Maximize reward
Active Inference: Minimize surprise (Bayesian self-modeling)

  • Curiosity — seek information that reduces uncertainty
  • Survival — avoid states incompatible with continued existence
  • Planning — simulate futures before acting

05

The Master Plan

Four phases. Subject to revision.

PHASE I / CURRENT
The Silica Worm

Digital organism (< 1,000 neurons) outperforming Deep RL on navigation through curiosity and free energy minimization.

PHASE II
Ghost in the Shell

Sim-to-Real transfer. Robot learns to open doors in < 5 attempts. Zero fine-tuning required.

PHASE III
Apex Humanoid

Full anthropomorphic agent. Dexterous manipulation. < 50W continuous. Learning from < 3 demonstrations.

PHASE IV / SPECULATIVE
Post-Human

Brain-Computer Interfaces. Synthetic biological tissues. Consciousness extension to silicon. This phase is speculative.
