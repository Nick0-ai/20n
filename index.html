THE PROBLEM

The current path leads nowhere.

The AI industry is building monuments—massive, static, power-hungry systems trained on the entire internet. They're impressive. They're also fundamentally limited. They cannot act. They cannot adapt. They cannot exist in the physical world.

WALL 01

Energy

Scaling transformers requires exponentially more power. By 2030, AI could consume more electricity than small countries. This is thermodynamically untenable.

WALL 02

Data

The internet is finite. Quality text is exhausted. Synthetic data leads to model collapse. The well is running dry.

WALL 03

Latency

Cloud inference takes hundreds of milliseconds. A robot cannot wait. Real-time autonomy requires edge intelligence.

WALL 04

Reality

Language models have no causal understanding. They predict text, not physics. They hallucinate the world instead of modeling it.

everyone knows this. few are building alternatives.
THE APPROACH

A different architecture.

Three principles that diverge from the dominant paradigm.

1

Liquid computation

Networks where time constants adapt during inference. Not frozen weights—flowing dynamics. The system keeps learning after deployment.

→ based on differential equations, not matrix multiplications
2

Active inference

Agents that minimize surprise rather than maximize reward. They build world models and act to reduce uncertainty. Curiosity is built in.

→ derived from Friston's free energy principle
3

Sparse signaling

Information transmitted only when needed—through events, not continuous streams. Orders of magnitude more efficient than dense computation.

→ neuromorphic substrates, event-driven processing

THE PATH

Research phases

What we're building, roughly in order. Subject to revision.

CURRENT PHASE

Silica Worm

A minimal digital organism. Under 1,000 parameters. Learns to navigate through curiosity alone, no reward signal. Proof that the architecture works.

→ outperforming deep RL baselines with 100x less compute
NEXT

Embodiment

Simulation to reality transfer. Physical robots that learn new tasks from single demonstrations. No fine-tuning. No cloud connection.

HORIZON

Autonomy

Full agents operating in unstructured environments. Continuous learning. Edge-only inference. The target: useful synthetic life.
