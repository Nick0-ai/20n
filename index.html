01 — THE PROBLEM

The scaling hypothesis is approaching four walls

The AI industry is accelerating toward terminal asymptotes. Everyone sees them coming. Few are building alternatives.

01

Energy

GPT-4 inference requires ~20 megawatts. The human brain uses 20 watts. A million-fold gap that scaling cannot close.

02

Data

The internet is finite. Quality text is exhausted. Synthetic data creates model collapse. The well is running dry.

03

Latency

Cloud inference takes 200-500ms. A robot cannot wait. Real-time autonomy requires edge intelligence.

04

Physics

LLMs have no causal model. They predict tokens, not reality. They hallucinate physics because they never experienced it.

02 — THE APPROACH

The 20n trinity

Three paradigms, unified. Not a framework—an organism architecture.

  • A
    Liquid Neural Networks
    Time constants that adapt during inference. Not frozen weights—flowing dynamics.
  • B
    Active Inference
    Minimize surprise, not reward. Agents that build world models and act to reduce uncertainty.
  • C
    Sparse Signaling
    Information transmitted only when needed. Orders of magnitude more efficient than dense computation.

04 — THE PATH

Four phases toward synthetic life

PHASE I — CURRENT

Silica Worm

Digital organism with <1,000 parameters. Learns through curiosity alone. Outperforming deep RL on navigation.

PHASE II

Embodiment

Sim-to-real transfer. Physical robots that learn from single demonstrations. No fine-tuning required.

PHASE III

Apex

Full anthropomorphic agents. Dexterous manipulation under 50W. Learning from fewer than 3 demos.

PHASE IV — SPECULATIVE

Post-Human

Brain-computer interfaces. Synthetic biological tissues. The boundary becomes irrelevant.
